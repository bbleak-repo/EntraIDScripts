Microsoft Lighthouse / GDAP -- MSP Supply Chain Security
Prepared 2026-03-30 | Cybersecurity Risk Perspective

1. Threat Landscape

Managed Service Providers occupy a uniquely dangerous position in the modern threat landscape. They hold privileged access to dozens, hundreds, or thousands of customer tenants simultaneously. A single compromised MSP credential is not equivalent to a single compromised enterprise account -- it is a key that opens many locks.

The Multiplicative Risk Problem

CISA Advisory AA22-131A (May 2022) documented the pattern: when a target has a mature security posture, attackers find it easier to first compromise the MSP serving that target, then pivot through delegated access. The MSP is not the ultimate target -- it is the attack path.

2. Breach Case Studies

SolarWinds SUNBURST
Discovered December 2020 | Active since March 2020
NATION-STATE
Attacker: NOBELIUM / Russian SVR Vector: Supply chain (trojanized Orion update) Impact: ~18,000 installations received malicious update

The SUNBURST backdoor was inserted into SolarWinds Orion software updates distributed between March and June 2020. The trojanized DLL was signed with SolarWinds' legitimate code-signing certificate. Notable victims included the US Treasury Department, Department of Homeland Security, and multiple federal agencies. The attack demonstrated that trusted vendor/MSP relationships could be weaponized at massive scale.

MSP Relevance: Demonstrated that the vendor/service provider relationship is a viable and high-value supply chain attack path. Directly motivated Microsoft's GDAP initiative.
Kaseya VSA / REvil Ransomware
July 2, 2021
RANSOMWARE
Attacker: REvil ransomware gang Vector: CVE-2021-30116 (zero-day in Kaseya VSA) Impact: 800-1,500 businesses across ~60 MSPs Ransom: $70 million demand (largest at the time)

REvil exploited a zero-day authentication bypass in Kaseya's VSA remote monitoring platform on the Friday before the US Independence Day weekend. The attack cascaded through approximately 60 MSPs to between 800 and 1,500 downstream businesses. A universal decryptor was obtained on July 23. Coop, Sweden's largest grocery chain, had to close nearly 800 stores due to compromised point-of-sale systems via their MSP.

MSP Relevance: Direct exploitation of an MSP management platform. Demonstrated the cascading blast radius of MSP compromise. Triggered a re-pricing of cyber insurance for MSPs.
ConnectWise ScreenConnect
February 19, 2024
MULTI-GROUP
Attackers: Multiple groups including Black Basta Vector: CVE-2024-1709 (CVSS 10.0 authentication bypass) Impact: Widespread exploitation of MSP remote access tool

A critical authentication bypass vulnerability (CVSS 10.0) was disclosed in ConnectWise ScreenConnect on February 19, 2024. Mass exploitation began within 24 hours of disclosure. Multiple threat groups including Black Basta leveraged the vulnerability to gain access to MSP-managed endpoints. The simplicity of exploitation (crafted HTTP request) and the widespread deployment of ScreenConnect made this one of the most rapidly exploited MSP vulnerabilities on record.

MSP Relevance: MSP remote management tooling as a direct attack surface. Speed of exploitation (hours, not days) emphasizes the need for rapid patching and network segmentation of management tools.
BerryDunn / Reliable Networks
September 2023
LIABILITY PRECEDENT
Attacker: Unknown threat actor Vector: Compromised MSP (Reliable Networks) credentials Impact: 1.1 million individuals' data exposed

BerryDunn, an accounting and consulting firm, suffered a data breach through their managed service provider, Reliable Networks. The breach exposed personal data of approximately 1.1 million individuals. BerryDunn subsequently filed litigation against Reliable Networks, establishing an important legal precedent for MSP liability in data breach scenarios. The case highlighted that contractual liability caps may not protect MSPs from negligence claims.

MSP Relevance: Legal liability precedent. Demonstrates that customers will pursue MSPs in court for security failures. SOW language, GDAP role alignment, and evidence of security controls become critical for legal defense.

4. Incident Response Playbook

Structured response for compromised MSP accounts accessing customer tenants via GDAP.

Phase 1: 0-30 Minutes

Immediate Containment

  • Disable compromised account in Entra ID
  • Revoke all active sessions and refresh tokens
  • Remove from ALL GDAP security groups
  • Document: timestamp, account, initial indicators
  • Note: 30-min propagation delay for group changes
Phase 2: 30 Min - 4 Hours

Investigation

  • Query Entra sign-in logs: CrossTenantAccessType = "serviceProvider"
  • Query Purview: UserType = 9 for compromised user
  • Identify all customer tenants accessed
  • Check for persistence: app registrations, new accounts, modified CA policies
  • Assess blast radius and data exposure
Phase 3: Notification

Customer Communication

  • GDPR: 72-hour notification window
  • HIPAA: 60-day notification window
  • State breach laws: varies by jurisdiction
  • Template: what happened, what data, remediation steps
  • Coordinate with customer's IR team
Phase 4: Remediation

Recovery & Hardening

  • Rotate all credentials and certificates
  • Review and harden all GDAP relationships
  • Implement additional controls identified during investigation
  • Conduct post-incident review
  • Update SOPs based on lessons learned
30-Minute Propagation Delay

After removing a user from GDAP security groups, there is a documented 30-minute window during which access may technically persist due to token caching and group membership propagation delays. Document the exact removal time in your incident record. Use the "Revoke sessions" command in Entra ID in parallel to force token invalidation.

6. Cyber Insurance Considerations

Post-Kaseya Market Reality

The Kaseya incident in July 2021 triggered a significant re-pricing of cyber insurance for MSPs. Underwriters have become increasingly specific about technical security controls, moving from questionnaire-based self-attestation to evidence-based verification.

Underwriting QuestionGDAP/Lighthouse Evidence
Is MFA enabled for all admin accounts? GDAP mandates MFA in partner tenant. Export CA policy config as evidence.
Are privileged access controls in place? PIM for Groups with tier model. Export PIM activation logs.
Is there an audit trail for admin actions? Entra audit logs + Purview UserType 9 + Lighthouse audit. Export samples.
Is there a documented IR plan? Incident response playbook (see Section 4). Annual testing evidence.
Are access reviews performed regularly? Quarterly GDAP relationship review. PIM access review campaigns.
Trend: Proof Required, Not Self-Attestation

Underwriters increasingly require evidence from logging systems and configuration exports -- not checkbox self-attestation. MSPs who cannot demonstrate CA policy configurations, PIM activation logs, and GDAP relationship documentation face coverage denial or substantially elevated premiums. First-party vs. third-party coverage distinction is critical: first-party covers the MSP's own losses; third-party covers liability to customers.

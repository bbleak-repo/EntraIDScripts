Section 01

Executive Overview

Microsoft 365 Lighthouse is the platform through which Managed Service Providers (MSPs) administer multiple customer Microsoft 365 tenants from a single pane of glass, covering identity, security posture, device management, and compliance monitoring. Prior to 2022, this access was granted via Delegated Admin Privileges (DAP) — a broad, unscoped mechanism that automatically conferred Global Administrator rights across every customer tenant with no time limit and no customer approval required.

The NOBELIUM supply chain attacks of 2021 demonstrated catastrophically that compromising a single MSP partner account provided threat actors with lateral movement paths into dozens or hundreds of downstream customers simultaneously. In direct response, Microsoft deprecated DAP in July 2024, replacing it with Granular Delegated Admin Privileges (GDAP): a time-bounded, role-scoped, customer-approved delegation model.

This document summarizes the current security architecture of Microsoft Lighthouse under GDAP, identifies residual risks that require organizational controls, and presents a prioritized set of recommendations for hardening the MSP access model to protect both the organization and its customers.

Section 04

DAP vs. GDAP — Before and After

DAP — Delegated Admin Privileges Deprecated July 2024
  • Automatic Global Administrator role granted to all customer tenants upon CSP relationship creation
  • No time limit — access persisted indefinitely without renewal or review
  • No role granularity — full tenant control or nothing
  • No customer approval required — unilateral partner-side activation
  • No dedicated audit logs for delegated access actions
  • Single partner account compromise exposed every downstream customer simultaneously
GDAP — Granular Delegated Admin Privileges Current Standard
  • Specific Entra ID roles assigned per customer — least privilege enforced at the role level
  • Time-bound relationships (1 to 730 days) with defined expiry and auto-extend increments
  • Customer must explicitly approve each GDAP relationship request
  • Per-customer scoping — a compromise in one partner account does not automatically extend to all customers
  • Full audit trail in Entra ID audit logs and Microsoft 365 unified audit log
  • Supports Privileged Identity Management (PIM) just-in-time elevation for partner security groups
Section 05

Security Architecture — Defense in Depth

1
GDAP Permission Model
Foundation of the access control model. Entra ID roles are scoped to specific customers and time-bounded. Role assignments align to service line requirements (e.g., Helpdesk Technician, Exchange Administrator, Intune Administrator) rather than broad Global Administrator grants.
Least Privilege Role Scoping Time-Bound Customer Approval
2
Conditional Access Policies
Partner home-tenant Conditional Access policies govern all delegated access sessions. Controls include phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2 / Windows Hello), compliant device requirements, and named-location geo-fencing restricting access to US-based IP ranges. Customer tenant CA policies do not apply to partner sessions.
Phishing-Resistant MFA Device Compliance Geo-Fencing Session Controls
3
Privileged Identity Management (PIM) / Just-In-Time Access
GDAP security groups in the partner tenant are configured as PIM-eligible rather than permanent. Technicians request time-limited elevation for specific tasks, subject to approval workflows and business justification. Activation scope in Lighthouse applies across all tenants in a role template — per-customer activation is not currently supported.
JIT Elevation Approval Workflows Justification Required Template-Level Scope (Risk)
4
Auditing & Monitoring
Microsoft Sentinel provides cross-tenant SIEM integration for comprehensive log correlation. Microsoft Purview Compliance Center provides detailed audit logs, though interactive audit search via GDAP is not supported — a dedicated audit-only account is required. Lighthouse native alerts cover Defender for Business, Defender Antivirus, and Entra ID identity protection events.
Microsoft Sentinel Purview Audit Lighthouse Alerts Purview GDAP Gap (Risk)
5
Operational SOPs & Governance
People and process controls that wrap the technical layers. Includes defined onboarding and offboarding procedures for both customers and technicians, quarterly access reviews, annual GDAP relationship audits, incident response runbooks for partner account compromise, and session recording via CyberArk PSM for privileged administrative sessions.
Access Reviews Onboarding / Offboarding Incident Response Session Recording
Section 06

Critical Risk Findings

Risk
GDAP MFA Trust Override — Partner Home Tenant Controls Are Always Authoritative
When a technician authenticates via GDAP, the partner home tenant’s MFA satisfaction is unconditionally trusted by all customer tenants — regardless of the customer’s own Conditional Access policies or MFA requirements. A customer organization cannot enforce stronger authentication standards on their MSP’s access than the MSP enforces on itself. This places the entire security burden on the partner tenant’s controls.
Risk
MSP Identity Redacted in Customer Audit Logs
Partner technician activity recorded in customer tenant audit logs appears as “[Partner Name] Technician” rather than the individual technician’s user principal name. This limits forensic traceability within the customer tenant. Detailed per-technician audit attribution requires access to the partner tenant’s own audit logs, which the customer cannot independently access.
Risk
Lighthouse PIM JIT Activates Across All Tenants in a Role Template
When a technician activates a GDAP role via PIM within the Lighthouse JIT workflow, the elevation applies to every customer tenant associated with that role template — not to a single specified customer. This is broader than the minimum necessary access principle for single-customer tasks and increases the blast radius of a compromised activation.
Risk
Microsoft Purview Interactive Audit Search Not Supported via GDAP
Compliance administrators operating under GDAP delegation cannot use the Microsoft Purview compliance portal’s interactive content search and audit tools. A dedicated, non-GDAP account with Compliance Administrator or eDiscovery permissions must be provisioned per customer tenant to perform Purview audit operations. This creates additional account management overhead and a potential orphaned-account risk if not tracked.
Risk
Lighthouse Native Alerting Has Limited Scope
Lighthouse’s built-in alert capabilities are limited to events from Microsoft Defender for Business, Microsoft Defender Antivirus, and Entra ID Identity Protection. Significant security event categories — including SharePoint/OneDrive data exfiltration, Exchange transport rule manipulation, Azure resource abuse, and third-party application consent grants — are not surfaced through Lighthouse alerts and require Microsoft Sentinel or equivalent SIEM integration for coverage.
Section 07

Key Recommendations

1
Critical
Complete DAP-to-GDAP migration immediately for any remaining customer relationships. DAP was deprecated in July 2024 and represents an unacceptable access control risk. Verify no DAP relationships remain active via Partner Center.
2
Critical
Implement PIM for Groups on all GDAP security groups in the partner tenant. Technicians must have no standing privileged access — all elevated roles must be time-limited, require justification, and trigger approval workflows. Permanent group membership for GDAP groups is unacceptable.
3
High
Deploy Conditional Access in the partner tenant requiring phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2 security keys or Windows Hello for Business) for all GDAP-enabled accounts. Password + SMS/TOTP MFA is insufficient given the privilege level of these accounts.
4
High
Configure US-only named location geo-fencing in the partner tenant’s Conditional Access policies to restrict GDAP access to known US IP ranges. Document the VPN bypass risk: technicians on VPN with non-US exit nodes may be blocked. Evaluate split-tunnel or IP allowlist approach for remote work scenarios.
5
High
Deploy Microsoft Sentinel with cross-tenant workspace configuration for comprehensive MSP security monitoring. Lighthouse’s native alerts are insufficient for detecting data exfiltration, lateral movement, or application-layer attacks. Sentinel is the minimum viable solution for enterprise-grade MSP security posture.
6
Medium
Implement CyberArk Privileged Session Manager (PSM) for session recording of all high-privilege administrative actions. PIM controls when access is granted, but does not record what was done during the session. Session recording is essential for audit defensibility and forensic investigation.
7
Medium
Align GDAP role assignments to Statement of Work service lines. Each customer’s GDAP relationship should grant only the roles required to deliver contracted services. Avoid assigning Global Administrator or broad roles as a convenience measure for future tasks.
8
Medium
Establish quarterly access reviews for all GDAP security group memberships and annual reviews of GDAP relationship scope per customer. Offboarded technicians and deprecated service lines should trigger immediate access revocation, not periodic review cycles.
